Evidence base & reference data sources

European normative curves Tideman et al. (2018)

Tideman JWL, Polling JR, Vingerling JR, Jaddoe VWV, Williams C, Guggenheim JA, Klaver CCW. Axial length growth and the risk of developing myopia in European children. Acta Ophthalmologica. 2018;96(3):301–309. doi:10.1111/aos.13603

Primary source for the European percentile bands. 12,386 participants across Generation R (Dutch children aged 6 and 9y; n=6,934), ALSPAC (British children 15y; n=2,495), and Rotterdam Study III (Dutch adults; n=2,957). Published anchor values: mean (SD) 22.36 (0.75) mm at age 6; 23.10 (0.84) mm at age 9; 23.41 (0.86) mm at age 15. Percentile bands in this tool are derived from these anchors using mean ± Z·SD, interpolated continuously between ages 6–18. Below-median percentiles flatten after 15y per the paper's findings.

East Asian normative curves Sanz Diez et al. (2019)

Sanz Diez P, Yang L-H, Lu M-X, Wahl S, Ohlendorf A. Growth curves of myopia-related parameters to clinically monitor the refractive development in Chinese schoolchildren. Graefe's Archive for Clinical and Experimental Ophthalmology. 2019;257(8):1045–1053. doi:10.1007/s00417-019-04290-6

Source for East Asian/Chinese percentile bands. 12,554 children (6,054 girls, 6,500 boys) aged 5–16 years from Wuhan, China (Lenstar LS 900). Chinese children show substantially longer axial lengths than European children — difference increasing with age (+0.32 mm at age 6, +0.47 mm at age 9, +1.84 mm at age 15 for males).

26 mm threshold IMI White Papers (2019)

Flitcroft DI, He M, Jonas JB et al. IMI — Defining and classifying myopia. Invest Ophthalmol Vis Sci. 2019;60(3):M20–M30. doi:10.1167/iovs.18-25957

Source for the 26 mm axial length threshold. High myopia defined as ≤−6.00 D, corresponding to approximately 26 mm AL. Above this threshold, risk of myopic maculopathy, retinal detachment, glaucoma, and cataract rises substantially.

UK clinical guidance College of Optometrists (2023)

Clinical Management Guidelines: Myopia in children and young people. London: College of Optometrists; updated 2023. college-optometrists.org

Informs the progression rate thresholds: slow ≤0.10 mm/yr; moderate 0.10–0.20 mm/yr; fast 0.20–0.30 mm/yr; very fast >0.30 mm/yr. Framework for escalation decisions.

Treatment efficacy Brennan et al. (2021)

Brennan NA, Toubouti YM, Cheng X, Bullimore MA. Efficacy in myopia control. Progress in Retinal and Eye Research. 2021;83:100923. doi:10.1016/j.preteyeres.2020.100923

Meta-analysis confirming mean untreated axial elongation in childhood myopia typically falls in the 0.20–0.30 mm/yr range, supporting the progression thresholds used in this tool.

Important limitations: Percentile curves are population references, not individual risk predictors. The Tideman (2018) data are available only at discrete ages (6, 9, 15 years); values between these ages are interpolated. Ethnic, geographic, and cohort variation means curves are indicative — clinical judgement is always required. This tool is a clinical aid and does not replace a full optometric examination and management plan. Patient records are stored locally in this browser only and are not transmitted anywhere.
